Christian Liberty Academy

Christian Liberty Academy is a private Christian school committed to providing a Bible-centered education that fosters academic excellence and spiritual growth. Located in Paramaribo, Suriname, it serves students from preschool through 12th grade.

Education Levels

Preschool

Our Preschool program introduces young children to a nurturing, faith-based environment where they learn foundational skills through play, stories, and creative activities.

Elementary School

Elementary students build strong academic foundations in reading, math, science, and social studies, all taught from a Christian worldview with an emphasis on character development.

Middle School

Our Middle School program encourages critical thinking, teamwork, and leadership, preparing students for the academic and social challenges of high school.

High School

High School students engage in a rigorous curriculum that prepares them for college and life, with opportunities for leadership, service, and spiritual growth.

About

Reformation Christian Ministries (RCM) began in 1979 when Rev. Geoffrey Donnan, along with his wife Nancy and their children, established a church and Christian school in Suriname. Initially planning only a one-year mission, the Donnans responded to the growing interest in Bible studies among English-speaking workers, which led to the founding of Open Door Bible Church and later the Christian Liberty Academy (CLA) in October 1979 with just 16 students.
In 1980, the ministry was formalized under Stichting Caribbean Christian Ministries. During that same year, Suriname experienced a military coup, and the church moved to the CLA property in Uitvlugt. Despite political instability, both the church and school expanded. When the Donnan family returned to the U.S. in 1986 for their children's education, local leaders continued the work under guidance from the U.S.
Leadership was gradually passed on to individuals such as Mitchell Persaud and Asgar Mohamed Hamid, who had been students and staff members at CLA. They were mentored for administrative and pastoral roles. The Orthodox Presbyterian Church supported the church for some time until it transitioned to the Covenant Reformed Presbyterian Church in 1998.
In 1994, Rev. Rudy Poettcker joined as a missionary and educator. He became pastor of First Presbyterian Church and later the headmaster of CLA. In 2002, he was succeeded as pastor by Rev. Asgar Hamid, who had completed theological studies through RCM.
A major setback occurred in 2003 when a fire destroyed the main school and church building, including the residence above. However, the community rebuilt the facility by 2005, allowing operations to continue smoothly.
Since 2006, Rev. Poettcker has been based in the U.S. but continues to serve as headmaster and field director, frequently traveling to Suriname. Today, both the church and school are thriving, self-sustaining institutions with strong local leadership and ongoing connections with RCM for educational and theological support.
